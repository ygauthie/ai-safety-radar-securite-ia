# Daily Brief (2026-04-13)

## Top 3 Developments

1. **Anthropic Restricts Access to Frontier Model "Mythos"** - [The policy surrounding Mythos marks an irreversible power shift](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/3MhJELzwpbR42xsJ3/the-policy-surrounding-mythos-marks-an-irreversible-power) signals the end of open access to state-of-the-art AI capabilities. Unlike every model since ChatGPT, Mythos will only be available to select companies through "Project Glasswing" for cybersecurity applications, marking what the author calls an "irreversible power shift" toward restricted access models.

2. **Claude Opus Performance Degradation Raises Reliability Concerns** - [Claude Opus 4.6's accuracy dropped from 83% to 68% on BridgeBench hallucination tests](https://twitter.com/bridgemindai/status/2043321284113670594), with [follow-up analysis](https://www.bridgebench.ai/hallucination) showing the model now hallucinates twice as frequently as when initially released. This degradation highlights critical questions about model reliability over time and deployment practices.

3. **AI Interpretability Methods Extended to Biomedical Models** - Research on [Sparse Autoencoders for Single-Cell Models](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/b3cvG387LKmbiBPaH/sparse-autoencoders-for-single-cell-models-1) applies mechanistic interpretability techniques from AI safety to biological foundation models, while [Nature Machine Intelligence published work on interpretability in biomedicine](https://www.nature.com/articles/s42256-026-01177-0), demonstrating the broader utility and cross-domain applications of AI safety methodologies.

## Risk Watch

**Increased Risks**: Claude Opus's [degraded performance and increased hallucination rates](https://www.bridgebench.ai/hallucination) signal potential reliability issues in deployed frontier models. The development of increasingly autonomous agents, including [bug investigation systems](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-cookbooks/pull/527) and [threat intelligence tools](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-cookbooks/pull/496), raises oversight and control concerns. [Instruction following degradation from ~100% to 0-30% accuracy](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-cookbooks/pull/528) in complex production prompts highlights deployment safety challenges.

**Decreased Risks**: The shift toward [restricted access models like Mythos](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/3MhJELzwpbR42xsJ3/the-policy-surrounding-mythos-marks-an-irreversible-power) may reduce certain misuse risks by limiting frontier capabilities to vetted organizations. Development of practical safety tools like [hallucination detection systems](https://github.com/XwhyZ-WHYLD/hfr0-muhalo/releases/tag/v0.1.0-dev) and [multilingual safety evaluations](https://github.com/wuyoscar/ISC-Bench/releases/tag/v0.0.4) improves defensive capabilities.

**Mixed Implications**: While restricted access reduces misuse risks, it also limits the research community's ability to conduct safety evaluations and develop alignment techniques, potentially slowing safety research progress when it's most needed.
