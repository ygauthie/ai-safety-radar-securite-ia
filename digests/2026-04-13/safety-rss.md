## Key Arguments

The Mythos policy analysis presents a significant governance argument about the trajectory of AI development. The author suggests we've crossed a threshold where the most capable AI systems will no longer be democratically accessible, instead being limited to "select few companies" through programs like the mentioned "project glasswing" for cybersecurity applications. This represents a potential shift from the relatively open access model that characterized the early phases of the current AI boom to a more controlled, restricted deployment approach.

This argument has important implications for AI safety governance, as it touches on questions of:
- Whether restricting access to the most capable models enhances safety through better control
- How concentration of AI capabilities among fewer actors affects the competitive landscape
- Whether this represents responsible scaling practices or concerning centralization of power
