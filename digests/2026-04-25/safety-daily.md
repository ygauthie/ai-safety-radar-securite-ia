## Top 3 Developments

**UK AI Safety Institute Develops New Methodology for Inferring LLM Propensities**: The [UK AI Safety Institute released a new methodology](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/g9FmhKL2vL45TuT9B/methodology-for-inferring-propensities-of-llms) for evaluating AI systems' tendencies toward undesired behavior, focusing on providing evidence for theoretical misalignment arguments rather than traditional red-teaming approaches. This matters because it represents a systematic advance in how government safety institutes assess AI risks before deployment, moving beyond reactive testing to proactive behavioral prediction.

**Claude Mythos Preview Autonomously Discovers Thousands of Software Vulnerabilities**: Anthropic's new model [autonomously discovered thousands of previously unknown software vulnerabilities](https://asiatimes.com/2026/04/claude-mythos-has-crossed-an-ai-red-line) and executed complete attack chains in controlled testing environments, finding decades-old flaws in software systems. This matters because it represents a significant capability jump in autonomous cyber operations that raises immediate dual-use concerns for critical infrastructure security and highlights urgent needs for governance frameworks around self-directing AI systems.

**Alignment Research Identifies Fundamental "Fantasia Problem"**: [New research demonstrates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21827v1) that modern AI alignment approaches assume users can clearly articulate their goals, but people often interact with AI systems before fully forming their intentions, creating systematic misalignment between training paradigms and actual human usage patterns. This matters because it reveals that standard alignment approaches may be fundamentally mismatched to human psychology, requiring new frameworks that account for evolving and incomplete user intentions.
