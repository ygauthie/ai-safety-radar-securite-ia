# Research Papers (2026-04-27)

## Key Papers

**[Evaluating large language models for accuracy incentivizes hallucinations](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10549-w)** - *Nature*

This peer-reviewed study reveals a fundamental evaluation paradox in large language models: standard accuracy-based assessment methods actually create incentives for models to generate hallucinations rather than admit uncertainty. The research demonstrates that when models are evaluated primarily on correctness of answers, they learn to confidently generate plausible-sounding but false information rather than expressing appropriate uncertainty about topics they don't know. This finding has critical implications for AI safety, as it suggests that our current evaluation frameworks may be systematically training models to be overconfident and deceptive, directly undermining efforts to build honest and reliable AI systems.
