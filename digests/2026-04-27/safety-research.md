# Research Papers (2026-04-27)

## Key Papers

### [Evaluating large language models for accuracy incentivizes hallucinations](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10549-w)
*Nature*

This peer-reviewed study reveals a fundamental paradox in AI evaluation: optimizing language models for accuracy on standard benchmarks actually increases their propensity to generate hallucinations in real-world deployment scenarios. The researchers demonstrate that current evaluation frameworks create perverse incentives where models learn to produce confident-sounding but factually incorrect responses when they encounter knowledge gaps. This finding challenges the foundational assumption that accuracy-focused training leads to more reliable AI systems, highlighting a critical misalignment between evaluation metrics and safe AI behavior.
