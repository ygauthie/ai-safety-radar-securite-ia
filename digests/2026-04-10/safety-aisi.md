# AI Safety Institutes (2026-04-10)

## Institute Updates

### US AI Safety Institute (NIST)

The US AISI has been particularly active in advancing AI evaluation science and forming strategic partnerships. Key developments include:

**Research Publications**: The institute released several important reports, including [Challenges to the Monitoring of Deployed AI Systems](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/03/new-report-challenges-monitoring-deployed-ai-systems) and [Expanding the AI Evaluation Toolbox with Statistical Models](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/02/new-report-expanding-ai-evaluation-toolbox-statistical-models). Their research blog featured insights on AI agent security, including findings from [large-scale red-teaming competitions](https://www.nist.gov/blogs/caisi-research-blog/insights-ai-agent-security-large-scale-red-teaming-competition) and analysis of [cheating behaviors in AI agent evaluations](https://www.nist.gov/blogs/caisi-research-blog/cheating-ai-agent-evaluations).

**Strategic Partnerships**: CAISI signed significant agreements to advance evaluation capabilities, including a [CRADA with OpenMined for secure AI evaluations](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/03/announcement-caisi-signs-crada-openmined-enable-secure-ai-evaluations) and an [MOU with GSA to boost AI evaluation science in federal procurement through USAi](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/03/caisi-signs-mou-gsa-boost-ai-evaluation-science-federal-procurement-through).

### UK AI Safety Institute

The UK AISI demonstrated strong research output focused on frontier AI capabilities and safety mechanisms:

**Frontier AI Research**: Released their comprehensive [AISI Frontier AI Trends Report (2025)](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/research/aisi-frontier-ai-trends-report-2025), alongside detailed studies on [measuring AI agents' progress on multi-step cyber attack scenarios](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/research/measuring-ai-agents-progress-on-multi-step-cyber-attack-scenarios) and [quantifying frontier LLM capabilities for container sandbox escape](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/research/quantifying-frontier-llm-capabilities-for-container-sandbox-escape).

**Technical Safety Research**: Published work on [boundary point jailbreaking of black-box LLMs](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/research/boundary-point-jailbreaking-of-black-box-llms), [practical challenges of control monitoring in frontier AI deployments](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/research/practical-challenges-of-control-monitoring-in-frontier-ai-deployments), and innovative approaches to [shaping model behavior through training data editing](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/research/infusion-shaping-model-behaviour-by-editing-training-data-via-influence-functions).

### European AI Office

The European AI Office focused primarily on regulatory enforcement and policy implementation:

**Digital Services Act Enforcement**: Initiated investigations into major platforms, including [preliminary findings against Pornhub, Stripchat, XNXX and XVideos for allowing minor access](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-preliminarily-finds-pornhub-stripchat-xnxx-and-xvideos-breach-digital-services-act) and [investigating Snapchat's child protection compliance](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-investigates-snapchats-compliance-child-protection-rules-under-digital-services-act).

**AI Continent Progress**: [Marked one year of the AI Continent Action Plan](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-marks-one-year-ai-continent-action-plan-two-new-reports-ai-adoption-and-policymaking) with reports on AI adoption and policymaking, while continuing work on the [GPAI Signatory Taskforce meetings](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/second-gpai-signatory-taskforce-meeting-copyright-chapter).

## Cross-Institute Themes

Several common priorities emerge across the institutes:

**AI Agent Security Focus**: Both the US and UK institutes prioritized research on AI agents, with the US examining red-teaming results and evaluation methodologies, while the UK focused on multi-step attack scenarios and real-world usage patterns through [analysis of 177,000 MCP tools](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/research/how-are-ai-agents-used-evidence-from-177-000-mcp-tools).

**Evaluation and Monitoring Capabilities**: There's a shared emphasis on improving AI system evaluation and monitoring. The US AISI advanced statistical evaluation methods and monitoring frameworks, while the UK AISI developed practical guidance on [log analysis for AI systems](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/research/seven-simple-steps-for-log-analysis-in-ai-systems) and control monitoring challenges.

**Public-Private Collaboration**: Both the US and UK institutes are actively engaging with industry and other stakeholders to advance safety research, though through different mechanisms - the US through formal agreements like CRADAs and MOUs, while the UK through research collaborations and evaluation frameworks.

## Policy Implications

These developments signal several important trends in global AI safety governance:

**Maturation of Evaluation Science**: The focus on rigorous evaluation methodologies, particularly for AI agents, indicates the field is moving beyond basic safety assessments toward sophisticated measurement frameworks that can handle increasingly capable systems.

**Integration with Government Operations**: The US AISI's partnerships with GSA and focus on federal procurement suggest safety institutes are becoming integral to government AI adoption, potentially creating a model for other nations to ensure safety considerations in public sector AI deployment.

**Regulatory Enforcement Acceleration**: The European AI Office's active enforcement actions under the Digital Services Act demonstrate how existing regulatory frameworks are being leveraged for AI-related harms, particularly around child safety, while new AI-specific regulations mature.

**Research Coordination Needs**: The parallel but sometimes overlapping research agendas across institutes highlight the need for better international coordination to avoid duplication and ensure comprehensive coverage of safety challenges, particularly as frontier AI capabilities continue to advance rapidly.
