# Hacker News Discussions (2026-04-10)

## Hot Topics

The AI safety community was dominated by five major developments this week:

1. **Anthropic's "Mythos" Model Crisis** - Multiple stories emerged around Anthropic's decision to withhold their new "Mythos" AI model from public release, citing safety concerns. [Anthropic says new AI model too dangerous for public release](https://thehill.com/policy/technology/5824219-anthropic-new-ai-dangerous-public/) sparked significant discussion, with community members analyzing [Anthropic's 244-page safety report](https://kuber.studio/blog/AI/Anthropic-Wrote-244-Pages-About-Their-AI-Model-That%27s-Too-Dangerous-To-Release.-I-Read-It-So-You-Don%27t-Have-To). The situation escalated to the point where [Treasury Secretary and Fed Chair convened bank CEOs about Mythos model risks](https://www.reuters.com/business/finance/bessent-powell-warn-bank-ceos-about-anthropic-model-risks-bloomberg-news-reports-2026-04-10/).

2. **OpenAI's UK Investment Withdrawal** - OpenAI's decision to [pull out of a landmark £31B UK investment package](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/apr/09/openai-pulls-out-of-landmark-31bn-uk-investment), specifically [putting Stargate UK on ice due to energy costs and red tape](https://www.theregister.com/2026/04/09/openai_puts_stargate_uk_on/), generated substantial discussion about the impact of regulatory environments on AI development.

3. **EFF Leaves X Platform** - While not directly AI safety-related, [EFF's departure from X](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/04/eff-leaving-x) garnered the highest engagement (1,331 points, 1,159 comments), reflecting broader concerns about platform governance and free speech that intersect with AI governance discussions.

4. **Legal Immunity Push by OpenAI** - [OpenAI's backing of a bill to exempt AI firms from harm lawsuits](https://www.wired.com/story/openai-backs-bill-exempt-ai-firms-model-harm-lawsuits/) raised immediate red flags in the safety community about accountability and liability frameworks.

5. **Anthropic's Legal and Financial Challenges** - [Federal Court denial of Anthropic's motion to lift 'Supply Chain Risk' label](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/08/technology/anthropic-pentagon-risk-circuit-court.html) highlighted ongoing tensions between AI companies and government oversight, even as [speculation about Anthropic's $100B+ valuation](https://futuresearch.ai/anthropic-30b-arr-ipo-valuation/) continues.

## Community Sentiment

The community appears deeply concerned about a potential "safety theater" versus genuine safety measures divide. The Mythos situation has created unusual alignment between financial regulators and AI safety advocates, suggesting the risks may be more concrete than typical capability discussions. There's notable skepticism about OpenAI's legal immunity push, with the minimal comment engagement (only 1 comment) suggesting either resigned cynicism or active avoidance of the topic.

The low engagement on many safety-critical stories (multiple stories with 0 comments) contrasts sharply with the 1,159 comments on EFF's platform departure, potentially indicating either discussion fatigue around AI safety topics or migration of serious safety discussions to more specialized venues.
