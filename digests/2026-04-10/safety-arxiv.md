# ArXiv - AI Safety Papers (2026-04-10)

## Key Papers

The latest AI safety research presents several significant developments across alignment, interpretability, governance, and risk assessment:

**[TraceSafe: A Systematic Assessment of LLM Guardrails on Multi-Step Tool-Calling Trajectories](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07223v1)** introduces the first comprehensive benchmark for evaluating safety guardrails during multi-step AI agent execution, addressing a critical gap as LLMs evolve into autonomous systems. This work highlights how traditional output-focused safety measures may be insufficient for agentic AI systems that interact with tools and external environments.

**[How to sketch a learning algorithm](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07328v1)** tackles the fundamental data deletion problem in AI interpretability, presenting methods to predict model behavior when specific training data is excluded. This addresses core questions about data influence on AI models that are essential for privacy, interpretability, and understanding model decision-making processes.

**[AgentCity: Constitutional Governance for Autonomous Agent Economies via Separation of Power](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07007v1)** addresses the "Logic Monopoly" problem - where autonomous AI agents operating across organizational boundaries create opaque collective behaviors beyond human oversight. The paper proposes constitutional governance frameworks inspired by democratic separation of powers to maintain human control over multi-agent systems.

**[Designing Safe and Accountable GenAI as a Learning Companion with Women Banned from Formal Education](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07253v1)** examines AI safety in high-risk, surveilled contexts where educational AI use could endanger users. This research highlights how AI safety considerations must extend beyond technical robustness to include user safety in oppressive environments.

**[A comparative analysis of machine learning models in SHAP analysis](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07258v1)** evaluates explainable AI methods across different model architectures, providing critical insights for deploying interpretable AI in high-stakes applications where understanding model decisions is essential for trust and accountability.

**[CSA-Graphs: A Privacy-Preserving Structural Dataset for Child Sexual Abuse Research](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07132v1)** presents novel approaches to enable AI safety research on harmful content while preserving privacy and legal compliance, demonstrating how sensitive AI safety research can proceed without exposing researchers or systems to harmful material.

**[Personalized RewardBench: Evaluating Reward Models with Human Aligned Personalization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07343v1)** advances pluralistic alignment research by providing systematic evaluation methods for reward models that must capture diverse human values and preferences, addressing a key challenge in developing AI systems that serve diverse populations.

## Trends

**Multi-Step Agent Safety**: A critical shift toward evaluating AI safety during execution trajectories rather than just final outputs, recognizing that autonomous agents present fundamentally different risk profiles than conversational AI systems.

**Governance for Multi-Agent Systems**: Growing attention to constitutional and institutional mechanisms for governing AI agent collectives, acknowledging that technical safety measures alone may be insufficient for complex multi-agent scenarios.

**Context-Sensitive Safety**: Increased recognition that AI safety requirements vary dramatically across deployment contexts, with particular attention to high-risk environments where AI use itself may endanger users.

**Interpretability Infrastructure**: Development of systematic frameworks and benchmarks for explainable AI, moving beyond ad-hoc interpretability toward standardized evaluation and deployment practices.

**Privacy-Preserving Safety Research**: Novel methodologies that enable AI safety research on sensitive domains without exposing systems or researchers to harmful content, addressing a key barrier to safety research advancement.

**Pluralistic Alignment Evaluation**: Sophisticated approaches to evaluating AI systems' ability to respect diverse human values and preferences, moving beyond single-objective optimization toward nuanced value alignment.
